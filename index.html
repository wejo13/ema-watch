How does this grid bot work?

What it does

The grid bot places a series of limit orders across a defined price range, alternating between buy orders below the current BTC price and sell orders above it. When a buy fills, a sell is placed one level higher. When a sell fills, a buy is placed one level lower. The bot collects the spread between levels repeatedly as long as price oscillates within the range.

Prerequisites

  • Your Lighter private key must be saved in Settings before starting.
  • Your account must have enough available margin — the bot shows you exactly how much is needed before you can start.

Configuration

FieldWhat it means
Lower boundThe lowest price level. Buy orders are placed down to this price.
Upper boundThe highest price level. Sell orders are placed up to this price.
Grid levelsNumber of orders spread evenly between bounds. More levels = tighter spacing = smaller profit per fill, but more fills. Minimum 2.
Notional per levelUSD size of each order. $500 = each order controls $500 of BTC at that level's price.
LeverageMargin multiplier. Default 50×. Lower leverage requires more capital but is safer.

Capital requirement

Required margin is calculated as:

Required margin = (levels × notional) × IMR × 1.05

IMR = 1 ÷ leverage (minimum 2% at 50×). The 1.05 adds a 5% safety buffer. If you don't have enough, the bot shows the maximum notional per level you can use.

How orders are placed

When you click Start, the bot calculates evenly spaced price levels between your bounds. Every level gets a limit order — sells above current price, buys below. Levels too close to current price are skipped to avoid instant fills. Orders are sent one by one to Lighter with a 120ms gap between each, with a unique ID per level so they can be individually cancelled.

After all grid orders are placed, two bound stop-loss orders are added at the edges of the range (see Stop-Loss below).

How fills work

The bot listens to Lighter's WebSocket for your account's order updates. When a fill is detected, the filled level is marked and a counter-order is placed one level in the opposite direction — buy filled → sell placed one level above; sell filled → buy placed one level below. Fill count and estimated P&L update in the stats panel.

Stop-loss

Two independent stop-loss mechanisms run in parallel:

  • Exchange-side SLs: Two reduce-only stop-loss orders are placed directly on Lighter at lower −0.5% and upper +0.5% when the bot starts. These trigger even if your browser is closed.
  • Client-side SL: The bot also watches live price in the browser. If price crosses either SL level, it immediately cancels all grid orders, cancels both SL orders, and sends a market close order — then stops the bot with status "SL Triggered".

Controls

ButtonWhat it does
StartValidates inputs, checks capital, places all orders, starts fill detection.
PauseStops counter-orders from being placed on new fills. Existing orders on Lighter stay open.
ResumeRe-enables counter-order placement after a pause.
StopCancels all open grid orders and both SL orders on Lighter. Does not automatically close any open position.

Price ladder visualization

  • Green lines — open buy orders
  • Red lines — open sell orders
  • Amber dashed line — current BTC price (live)
  • Grey dashed lines — pending or filled levels
  • Red dotted lines — SL boundaries (±0.5% from bounds)

State persistence

The bot saves its full state to your browser's local storage. If you close and reopen the page while the bot was running, it restores automatically, reconnects both WebSockets, and resumes monitoring. Orders already placed on Lighter remain active.

⚠️ Note: The P&L shown is an estimate based on price step × BTC size per fill — not realized P&L from Lighter. Fill detection and counter-order logic are functional but not yet extensively battle-tested in live conditions. Always monitor your first sessions manually.

How this strategy works
Entry: previous 4H candle closes below the 200 EMA, current candle is bullish (close > open) and closes back above the EMA — a reclaim from below. Entry price is the close of that candle.
Stop loss: set as a % below entry (adjustable below) — this distance equals 1R.
Take profit: tested independently at 1R through 10R, plus 20R, 50R, and 100R. Each TP level is a separate full simulation — trades aren't partially closed.
Cooldown: after a stop loss, no new entries for 20 candles. Only one trade open at a time. Trades still open at the end of the data are marked "Open" and excluded from win rate.
Bull Market Data: runs the strategy only inside the selected bull run window(s), skipping everything outside them. The 200 EMA stays continuous (computed on the full historical data) so it's accurate from day one of each window — only entries/exits are restricted to the chosen period(s).
Balance & P&L: set a starting balance and risk % per trade. Without compounding, each trade risks a fixed $ amount (risk % × starting balance) regardless of current balance. With compounding on, risk is recalculated as risk % of the balance at that point in time, so wins and losses scale with account growth.
